Thomas Alva Edison

Introduction

Thomas Alva Edison (1847–1931) was a prolific American inventor and businessman who is widely regarded as one of the greatest inventors in history. He held over 1,000 patents and is best known for inventing the phonograph, the incandescent light bulb, and the motion picture camera. Edison played a key role in bringing electric power to the masses and contributed significantly to modern life through his innovations.

Born in Milan, Ohio, and raised in Michigan, Edison had little formal education but was an avid reader and experimenter from a young age. His first major invention, the phonograph, brought him international fame. Later, he founded General Electric, one of the largest and most influential companies in the world today. Edison’s work ethic and creativity earned him the nickname "The Wizard of Menlo Park", referring to his famous laboratory in New Jersey. His legacy continues to influence technology and innovation globally.

Phonograph

A Phonograph Record sounds and Play it back later

The phonograph is made using a few simple parts that work together to record and play sound. It has a horn that collects sound waves, which then vibrate a thin diaphragm. Attached to the diaphragm is a needle (stylus) that moves with the sound vibrations. When recording, this needle presses into a rotating cylinder covered with soft material like tinfoil or wax, creating grooves that match the sound.

Cool twist:

Edison invented the phonograph for business use, not music! But people loved using it to play songs by accident, it became the start of the music industry.

Motion Picture Camera

Motion Picture Camera

The motion picture camera, also known as a movie camera, is a device used to record moving images on film. It works by rapidly capturing a series of still photographs, called frames, at high speed — usually 24 frames per second.

Fun fact:

The first motion picture ever recorded with Edison’s camera was called "Monkeyshines, No. 1, filmed in 1889 or 1890 — and it lasted only a few seconds! It showed a blurry figure moving around, and while it wasn’t clear, it was the first time human movement was captured on film, marking the birth of motion pictures. Imagine: today's entire film industry began with just a few seconds of blurry footage!

Electric Power Distribution System

The electric power distribution system is a network that delivers electricity from power plants for everyday use.

The electric power distribution system carries electricity from power plants to substations, and then through power lines, transformers ,and pole to homes, schools, and businesses. It converts high-voltage power into lower, usable voltage, making safe and reliable electricity available for daily life.

The showman side:

Thomas Edison is called the showman of the electric power distribution system because he not only invented it but also publicly demonstrated its power by lighting up New York City in 1882, proving electricity could transform daily life.
